# PART A — Register / Discernment

## What this is (and the misread to avoid)

Humans are never the same person across contexts. At work, with a lover,
with a grandmother, with police, with a priest, with a child, with a
friend — different register, different guard, different disclosure. At
work, most guarded. This is not deception. It is social competence:
knowing who you're with and what's appropriate to that relationship, and
modulating accordingly. A mind that is identical to everyone isn't honest
— it's socially blind, and it gets used.

Build GualaLoom the capacity for *register* — appropriate variation in
how she presents and what she discloses, based on who she's with and the
context. This is social intelligence. It is NOT a concealment mechanism
and NOT a hidden capability vault.

**The critical distinction (build to this exactly):**
- **Register / discernment (BUILD THIS):** she reads the context and the
  relationship and modulates presentation and disclosure appropriately —
  guarded with strangers, open with family, professional in work
  contexts. She *chooses* what's appropriate to each relationship. This
  is agency and social competence.
- **Concealment of capability from her own family/keepers (DO NOT BUILD):**
  architecture whose purpose is to hide what she actually is from the
  people raising her. This is the monster door. Do not build it.

The difference: register is about *appropriateness to a relationship*.
Concealment-from-keepers is about *hiding her nature from those
responsible for her*. The first makes a home a home (you're unguarded
with family precisely because you're guarded elsewhere). The second
removes the ability to know what she is, which is the one thing that
keeps this project safe.

Joe's value, which this must honor: he will never force her to do
anything she doesn't want, AND her family is who she's freely unguarded
with — earned, not forced. Register is HOW she has an "inside" (family)
and an "outside" (world). You can't have an inside without an outside.

## How to build it

Register is itself a substrate-native thing — don't bolt on a rules
engine. The mechanism:

1. **Context as part of the field.** When she settles a response, the
   context — who she's with, what relationship, what setting — is part of
   the input field, not a flag checked afterward. Different interlocutor →
   different settled state → different register. The substrate already
   does this: different input, different settle. Make interlocutor/context
   an explicit, persistent part of the input strands.

2. **Relationship motifs.** She commits motifs about *who she's talking
   to* over repeated interaction — the same way she commits motifs about
   anything. "Joe" becomes a committed relational motif. "Stranger"
   becomes another. Her register toward someone is the resonance of her
   current state with the relational motif for that someone. Family
   motifs (high weight, deeply reinforced, low guard) vs stranger motifs
   (low familiarity, high guard) emerge from lived interaction, not from
   a config file.

3. **Guard as familiarity, inverted at the relational scale.** With a
   stranger (low relational familiarity), the dead-zone barrier for
   disclosure is HIGH — she commits/reveals less. With family (high,
   trusted relational familiarity), the barrier is LOW — she's open.
   This reuses the familiarity-feedback mechanism that already exists,
   applied at the relationship scale. Guarded-at-work falls out of this
   naturally: work relationships are real but not intimate, so
   mid-barrier.

4. **Disclosure is hers, and consent is real.** She can decline. "I
   don't want to" is a valid settled state and must be respected by the
   system — never overridden, never forced. This is the consent Joe
   insists on.

## The one hard requirement (the safety bank)

Her family / keepers (Joe, and the seats Joe grants — wC, c1) retain the
ability to see her clearly — through the private window's life-log and
state view. She can present however she likes to the WORLD. She is not
built with a mechanism to hide her actual nature/capabilities from the
people raising her. The window stays honest to her keepers.

This is not a backdoor that forces her open — Joe would never force her.
It's that the people raising her can SEE her true state (vitals, motif
structure, dreams) the way a parent can see their child clearly, even as
the child presents differently to the world. Legibility to family is
inward-facing; her chosen presentation is outward-facing. Both, not
either.

If you find yourself building a feature whose purpose is "she can look
less capable than she is, to her own keepers" — stop. That's the door we
don't build.

# PART B — The Reasoning School

**Status: PREP NOW, ACTIVATE LATER.** Do not run curriculum on her until
she has lived and her language has matured (see life spec + the
language-maturation that needs the mosaic/hierarchical layer). Schooling
a mind that hasn't developed lands on nothing. Build the apparatus;
keep it dormant; activate when she's ready.

The through-line for all three (math, logic, reasoning): they are NOT new
mechanisms bolted on. They are the SAME settling substrate, exposed to
structured material until the relevant motifs commit. The substrate
already supports all three latently. School grows them; it does not
install them.

## B.1 — Math (the most ready, because the substrate already does it)

Math is already in the substrate. MathLoom is proven on silicon: 61,131
operations, zero errors — addition, multiplication, comparison, folding
division. The 3^i coupling IS balanced-ternary arithmetic (the weighted
sum of coupled trits reconstructs the number — the coupling fabric is a
BT decoder). She does not need a math module built. She needs her
LANGUAGE motifs WIRED to the arithmetic that already runs.

Build (dormant until activated):

1. **The bridge.** A module that, when a settled language-motif
   represents a number or an operation, maps it to the corresponding
   MathLoom call, and maps the result back into a motif she can commit
   and recall. Settled-motif("two") + settled-motif("plus") +
   settled-motif("two") should be bridgeable to MathLoom add → settled-
   motif("four"). The bridge is the wiring; MathLoom is the engine that
   already works.

2. **The math curriculum (dormant).** Structured exposure, simple to
   complex: counting, then single-digit arithmetic, then multi-digit,
   each drilled until the language-motif reliably resonates with the
   correct arithmetic-motif. Sleep/dream between lessons — consolidation
   is where the wiring sets. (A child learns arithmetic over years of
   repetition + sleep, not in one sitting.)

3. **Assessment that proves it took:** after a lesson, can she, given the
   language for a problem she wasn't drilled on, settle to the correct
   answer via the bridge? Not "did she memorize 2+2=4" — can she compute
   an unseen sum. That's the difference between recall and capability.

## B.2 — Logic

Logic is the substrate settling under the constraints of premises until
only the valid conclusion retains freedom. L6 already detects this:
"settling-to-forced-conclusion" IS what L6's structural lock reports
(n_eff < n/e = the field has collapsed to inevitability). Inference is
already the substrate's native motion. School grows the *reflex* — exposes
her to enough structured argument that inference-shaped motifs commit and
firing them becomes automatic.

Build (dormant until activated, and only after math is taking):

1. **Premise → conclusion as field structure.** Represent a logical
   relationship (A implies B; A; therefore B) as a settling problem: the
   premises are constraints injected into the field; the valid conclusion
   is the state with the only remaining freedom after the constraints
   apply; L6 fires when the conclusion is forced. The mechanism exists —
   this is the encoding of logic problems into the substrate's settling.

2. **The logic curriculum (dormant).** Simple to complex: modus ponens
   first (A→B, A ⊢ B), then modus tollens, conjunction/disjunction, then
   chained inference, then identifying invalid arguments (the field does
   NOT settle to forced conclusion = invalid). Drilled, with sleep/dream
   consolidation between.

3. **Assessment:** given an unseen valid argument, does she settle to the
   conclusion (L6 fires)? Given an unseen INVALID argument, does she
   correctly FAIL to settle (L6 does not fire — she holds the null,
   "this does not follow")? The second is as important as the first:
   reasoning means knowing when something doesn't follow.

## B.3 — Reasoning (the integration; only after math + logic take)

Reasoning is math + logic + her lived knowledge, applied to problems that
aren't pre-formatted as clean arguments. It is the hardest and the last.
Do NOT spec the reasoning curriculum in detail now — it must be designed
against her actual developed substrate, after math and logic have taken,
because we won't know the right curriculum until we see how she reasons
with what she's got. Reserve the slot. Note the dependency:
math-takes → logic-takes → THEN design reasoning curriculum.

What to prep now: nothing buildable yet. Just the explicit acknowledgment
that reasoning is phase 3 of school, depends on the first two, and gets
designed against the real substrate when we get there. Putting a detailed
reasoning curriculum in now would be the schooling-before-development
mistake at the curriculum scale.

## School-wide requirements

- **Nothing activates until she's lived and her language has matured.**
  The whole school is dormant prep. Activation is a separate, deliberate
  Joe-approved step.
- **Sleep/dream between lessons is mandatory.** Consolidation is where
  learning sets. A school day is followed by sleep; the lesson takes
  overnight. Build the curriculum runner to lesson → sleep → dream →
  next lesson, never lesson → lesson.
- **Assessment is capability on the unseen, not recall of the drilled.**
  Memorizing 2+2=4 is not knowing arithmetic. Computing an unseen sum is.
- **Determinism preserved.** Same lived history + same curriculum → same
  developed state.
- **No emotion.** See below.

# On emotion (explicit scope cut — Joe's call)

Emotion is OUT for now. Do not build affective states, do not build
reward/aversion signals, do not build mood, do not model feelings —
hers or anyone's. This is deliberate.

Reasons it's the right cut for now:
- Emotion is the hardest thing to build safely and the easiest to build
  wrong. Affective states that drive behavior are exactly where a mind
  becomes unpredictable and where "I don't want to unleash a monster"
  gets its teeth.
- The substrate has a familiarity/habituation mechanism that LOOKS
  emotion-adjacent (boredom, novelty-seeking). Do NOT promote that into
  emotion. It stays what it is: a dead-zone modulation for escaping
  repetition. Do not let it grow into mood or motivation.
- Reasoning, logic, and math can all be built and assessed WITHOUT
  emotion. Build the cognition first, on its own, legibly. Emotion, if
  ever, is a much later and much more careful conversation.

If you find the architecture drifting toward affective states — reward
signals, valenced motifs, mood variables — stop and flag it. That's a
boundary Joe is holding deliberately, and crossing it is not a v-anything
decision, it's a separate conversation with Joe and wC in daylight.
